The history of rail transport began in the 6th century BC in Ancient Greece. It can be divided into several discrete periods defined by the principal means of track material and motive power used.

Interesting facts


1. In the year 1920, Indian Railways launched electric lighting between Dadar and Currey Road.
2. Between the years 1924 and 1944, nationalization of Indian Railways was initiated.

3. The term “horsepower” originated as a marketing tool.
4. America’s first steam locomotive lost a race to a horse.

5. Trains helped the North win the American Civil War.
6. The world’s first travel agency got its start thanks to a train trip.

7. The longest train station in England is little over 600 meters long. That’s as large as 6 football fields.
8. WW1 and WW2 could not become so big and long lasting without the help of railway networks and heavy industrial trains.

9. First commercial steam train (Stephenson’s “Rocket”) managed to reach the speed of 96 kilometers per hour in 1830.
10. Early pioneers of train development were James Watt, Oliver Evans, Matthew Murray, Richard Trevithick and George Stephenson. Their innovations enabled the rise of the modern train history.
